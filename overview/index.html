Research Overview

My research focuses on optics and photonics, particularly the design and optimization of laser cavities for structured beams. Recently, I have been working on metasurfaces optical devices, especially achromatic metalenses. My work is applying new optical elements such as metasurfaces into traditional optical system to improve the performance. The electromagnetic simulation sotfwares (MATLAB, COMSOL) and ray-based optical design sotfwares (OSLO, ZEMAX Optic Studio) are used to simulate and optimizate the system.

Fields of Interest

  • Laser cavity for structured light (Vortex and Nondiffracting beams).
  • Metasurfaces and metalenses for broadband achromatic imaging and UV photonics.
  • Optical system design and optimization (hybrid metasurface–traditional optics).

Experience

  • Postdoctoral Researcher, Applied Optics Laboratory, NCKU — Achromatic metalens design in UV (September 2024–present).
    • Leading projects on achromatic metalens design in the UV regime, with emphasis on dispersion engineering and chromatic aberration correction.
    • Using COMSOL Multiphysics and MATLAB to model meta-atom libraries and system-level optical performance.
  • Ph.D., Physics, NCKU — Dual‑phase‑modulation digital lasers for structured beams (2020–2024).
    • Designed and constructed digital laser cavities integrating intra-cavity spatial light modulators (SLMs).
    • Generated and experimentally validated structured beams: vortex, nondiffracting, Mathieu–Gauss, and Hermite–Gaussian arrays.
    • Developed MATLAB codes for mode simulations, digital holography beam analysis, and mode purity measurement.
